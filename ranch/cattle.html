Role in Agriculture


Cattle are integral to modern agriculture. They serve as a vital source of meat, milk, leather, and other by-products. Dairy cattle are bred specifically for milk production, supplying a variety of dairy products such as milk, cheese, and yogurt.

Breeds


Cattle come in a wide range of breeds, each with its own unique characteristics and attributes. From the iconic black-and-white Holsteins known for their high milk production to the muscular Angus prized for their flavorful beef, there's a breed suited for almost every agricultural need.

Lifecycle and Husbandry

The lifecycle of cattle typically begins with breeding, followed by gestation, calving, and raising the young. Cattle require proper care and management throughout their lives. This includes access to nutritious feed, clean water, adequate shelter, and routine veterinary care. Farmers and ranchers employ various husbandry practices to ensure the health, welfare, and productivity of their cattle herds.

Breeding and Genetics:

Selective breeding and genetic improvement programs play a crucial role in cattle farming. These techniques enhance desirable traits such as growth rate, meat quality, milk production, disease resistance, and environmental adaptability. Artificial insemination (AI), embryo transfer, and genetic testing are commonly used techniques to propagate superior genetics within cattle populations.


Cattle are well-suited to graze on a variety of forages, including grasses, legumes, and browse. Pasture-based systems allow cattle to roam freely and graze on natural vegetation, while supplemental feeding with hay, silage, grains, and protein supplements may be necessary, especially during periods of scarcity or to meet specific nutritional requirements.





Pasture Management

Cattle farming can have both positive and negative environmental impacts, depending on management practices and scale. Sustainable grazing and pasture management practices can improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. Improper land management and overgrazing may lead to soil erosion, habitat degradation, and greenhouse gas emissions.

Environmental Sustainability

Our lush pastures provide the perfect backdrop for grazing cattle to thrive. Through sustainable pasture management practices, we prioritize soil health, biodiversity, and grassland productivity. Rotational grazing, holistic land management, and conservation initiatives ensure that our land remains fertile and vibrant for generations to come.

Animal Welfare

The well-being of our cattle is paramount at Delos. We adhere to the highest standards of animal care and husbandry, providing our cattle with access to clean water, nutritious forage, and spacious living conditions. Regular veterinary care, vaccination protocols, and humane handling practices are integral to maintaining the health and happiness of our herd.
