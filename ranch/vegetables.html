At Delos, every meal is a celebration of freshness, flavor, and abundance. These five vegetables showcase just a glimpse of the variety and abundance grown in Delos.

Tomatoes


Tomatoes come in various shapes, sizes, and colors. They can be juicy and sweet or tangy and acidic, making them a staple in many cuisines worldwide. Tomatoes are rich in antioxidants like lycopene, vitamins A and C, and potassium. They can be enjoyed raw in salads, sandwiches, or salsas, or cooked in sauces, soups, and stews.

Carrots


Carrots are crunchy, sweet, and packed with beta-carotene. They also contain fiber, vitamins C and K, and potassium. Carrots can be enjoyed raw as a healthy snack, grated into salads, roasted with honey and herbs, or pureed into soups and sauces. They add color, flavor, and nutrition to a wide range of dishes.

Bell Peppers


Bell peppers are crisp, sweet, and come in a range of vibrant colors, including red, yellow, orange, and green. They are an excellent source of vitamins A and C. Bell peppers can be enjoyed raw as crunchy snacks, sliced in salads, stuffed with savory fillings, or roasted and added to pasta dishes or stir-fries.

Zucchini


Zucchini, also known as courgette, is a summer squash with a mild flavor and tender texture. It's rich in vitamins A and C, potassium, and antioxidants. Zucchini can be sliced and grilled, sautéed with garlic and herbs, spiralized into noodles, or baked into bread and muffins. Its versatility makes it a favorite ingredient in both savory and sweet dishes.

Spinach


Spinach is a nutrient-rich leafy green vegetable packed with vitamins A, C, and K, iron, calcium, and antioxidants. It has a mild, slightly sweet flavor and a tender texture. Spinach can be eaten raw in salads, wilted into pasta dishes, blended into smoothies, or sautéed with garlic and olive oil as a side dish.
